  • What is CBD?

    CBD is short for cannabidiol. It is one of the 104 beneficial compounds found in the cannabis plant. The hemp plant, in its natural state, contains no CBD. Instead, it contains a full cannabidiol profile of CBDA, CBC, CBG, CBN, etc. CBD is essentially created by degrading these quality cannabidiols through a heat process called decarboxylation.

  • How does CBD work?

    Along with being a natural anti-inflammatory, possessing natural anti-anxiety qualities, providing natural pain relief and many more extraordinary qualities, CBD has a very similar molecular structure to that found in the human endocannabinoid system - which is made up of endocannabinoids and their receptors. These are found everywhere in the body, from the brain and other organs to glands and immune cells. In a nutshell, by helping the brain better understand issues within our bodies, we can help it do the healing for us.

  • What are terpenes?

    Terpenes are basically the essence oils of the plant. Terpenes are extremely complex elements and vary from one hemp strain to another. Terpenes can have healing qualities beyond (or greater than) those of CBD.

  • Full Spectrum vs. Broad Spectrum vs. Isolates

    Full Spectrum: These oils are of the highest quality and ideally leave the oils in the natural state provided by the plant or the “entourage effect.” These oils should contain the full cannabidiol profile (CBDA, CBC, CBG, CBN, etc.) and should also contain the natural terpenes, the small amount of THC in the hemp plant, some chlorophyll, and other beneficial organic material from the plant. Our hemp oil contains about 70% of the CBD profiles and the remaining contents are the other beneficial elements.

    Broad Spectrum: This is a new term that seems to relate to oil that undergoes extra processing to eliminate the THC and other elements. We have found that terpenes and CBDA get degraded through the excess processing.

    Isolates: Highly processed oil that degrades the full spectrum cannabidiol profile to create an oil or powder 96+% fully decarboxylated CBD. This is usually done to large scale industrial hemp with undesirable terpenes.

    So what does all this mean? Full spectrum oils will vary in quality from one harvest to the next, though ours will always fluctuate at the high end of the quality scale. Isolates will be more consistent, but much further down on the quality scale, and broad spectrum falls somewhere in between.

  • What is the entourage effect?

  • Hemp Flower Oil vs. Hemp Seed Oil

    Hemp flower oil or “CBD oil” is extracted from the hemp flower. This is the oil we grow and use in all our products. Hemp seed oil is pressed from the hemp seed. It is high in complex B vitamins, but does not contain CBD. Hemp flower oil costs about 500x more than hemp seed oil. Don’t be fooled by companies claiming hemp seed oil as part or all of the volume of CBD oil or “hemp extract” in their products.
